ANALOGUE FILM PRACTICE

Photochemical work, lab-based processes and technical experimentation with film.

This section gathers ongoing and past work developed with analogue film – from photochemical manipulation and contact printing to documentation of workflows, tests and lab practices that respond to the material conditions of film as a medium.

Analogue Film Practice

Photochemical Techniques

Notes, tests and projects developed through photochemical processes: hand-processing, tinting and toning, contact printing, optical and chemical interventions on the film strip. This includes experiments with colour separation, masking, exposure variations and the interaction between light, emulsion and chemistry.

Analogue Film Practice

Technical Documentation

Documentation of workflows, setups and procedures: from camera and projector configurations to lab protocols, testing charts, exposure notes and workflow diagrams. This space is dedicated to collecting the technical memory of each project – how things were done, what worked, what failed, and what can be repeated or revisited.

Analogue Film Practice

Darkroom / Lab Work

Work carried out in the lab and darkroom: processing sessions, print tests, densitometry, enlarger setups and small-scale lab routines. This includes reflections on working conditions, improvisation with limited resources and the physical labour involved in sustaining analogue film practices today.
