Based in Lisbon, Portugal

Mariana (Lisbon, 1997) is an archivist and film practitioner dedicated to the preservation of analog film techniques and technologies, with a particular focus on resisting technological obsolescence. Holding a background in Film Studies from ESTC Lisbon and a postgraduate degree in Audiovisual Archiving from Elías Querejeta Zine Eskola, her work spans archival research, material film practices and artistic experimentation.

She has collaborated with institutions such as the Cinemateca Portuguesa and Cineric P., contributing to digital migration, film duplication and inspection processes, and has assisted artist and filmmaker Salomé Lamas on multidisciplinary projects. Her archival path includes the “Z-A Filmmakers' Archive”, dedicated to the work of Portuguese avant-garde artist Ernesto de Sousa, which led to a research grant at Cinemateca Portuguesa in 2024.

Mariana is also involved in international research projects and collectives, focusing on non-aligned film archives, countercultural film publications and the circulation of militant cinema. Her practice unfolds at the intersection of Third Cinema memory, film materiality and archival resistance through technological practice.

Film performance · 16mm

Send Me a Color

Vimeo · https://vimeo.com/1081106970

A filmic performance that explores the mechanical gestures of 16mm projectors and the materiality of light and color. Through duplication processes and the use of prisms and filters, the work revisits chromatic separation techniques to create a dense field of chromatic variations where analog color becomes movement, vibration and afterimage.

On screen, thousands of chromatic possibilities unfold through crystals and filters, exploring refraction, reflection and the fragile thresholds between abstraction and figure. The piece was developed with institutional support from different cultural partners and presented in performance and screening contexts.

Digital Restoration

La Cruz de Mayo – Digital Restoration

Vimeo · https://vimeo.com/1084541446

Collaborative digital restoration carried out with the Archivistas Salvajes collective, dedicated to recovering the images of La Cruz de Mayo (1983), directed by Miguel Secades and Miguel García in Cuba. The work respects the materiality and limitations of the original format while improving stability, contrast and legibility.

Beyond its aesthetic power and revolutionary spirit, the film’s resonance is deeply rooted in the technical and political conditions under which it was produced. The restoration process becomes a way of reactivating this memory and opening the work to new publics and contexts of exhibition and discussion.
