About

Background, archival trajectory and current lines of work.

Mariana (Lisbon, 1997) is an archivist and film practitioner dedicated to the preservation of analogue film techniques and technologies, with a particular focus on resisting technological obsolescence. With a background in Film Studies from ESTC Lisbon and a postgraduate degree in Audiovisual Archiving from Elías Querejeta Zine Eskola, her work spans archival research, material film practices and artistic experimentation.

She has collaborated with institutions such as the Cinemateca Portuguesa and Cineric P., contributing to film inspection, duplication and digital migration workflows. She has also worked in festival environments, providing technical support for 35mm, 16mm and DCP projection.

Her practice unfolds at the intersection of Third Cinema memory, film materiality and archival resistance, often moving between the film lab, the projection booth and the reading room.

Artistic Works
Send Me a Color
16mm film performance · Photochemical experimentation

Vimeo · https://vimeo.com/1081106970

A filmic performance exploring the mechanical gestures of 16mm projectors and the materiality of light and colour. Through duplication processes and the use of prisms, filters and chromatic separation techniques, the work turns analogue colour into vibration, flicker and afterimage.

Research & Preservation Works
La Cruz de Mayo – Digital Restoration
Digital restoration · Archivistas Salvajes collective

Vimeo · https://vimeo.com/1084541446

Collaborative digital restoration of La Cruz de Mayo (1983), focusing on stabilisation, contrast and legibility while respecting the material limitations of the original. The work engages with the political and technical conditions under which the film was produced, activating its memory for contemporary audiences.

Ernesto de Sousa – “A Quinta Experimental, Uma Nova Terra…”
Archival research · Cinemateca Portuguesa

Research on the unfinished project by Ernesto de Sousa, originally conceived as a promotional film for Shell centred on a “pilot farm” in Santarém. Working with script drafts, research notes and unedited footage preserved at the Cinemateca Portuguesa, the project reads these fragments as traces of the film The Promised Land, revealing the tensions between capitalist modernisation, rural life and experimental cinema.

Analogue Film Practice

Focused on photochemical techniques, technical documentation and darkroom / lab work.

Photochemical Techniques

Work with 16mm and 35mm film including inspection, splicing, manual repairs and preparation for scanning and projection. This includes experience with ArriScan and custom Cineric scanners, wet-gate workflows and RGB separation for preservation.

  • Film inspection (perforations, emulsion damage, shrinkage, splices).
  • Preparation of reels for scanning and projection.
  • Basic duplication and printing workflows in collaboration with lab technicians.
  • Use of prisms, filters and optical devices for colour separation and recomposition.
Technical Documentation

Documentation of workflows, scanner settings, file structures and quality control procedures for archival projects. Emphasis on clarity, reproducibility and long-term legibility of technical decisions.

  • Scanner logs, capture parameters and workflow diagrams.
  • File naming conventions, directory structures and metadata notes.
  • QC reports describing artefacts, fixes and pending issues.
Darkroom / Lab Work

Lab-oriented practices involving handling of light-sensitive materials, controlled environments and collaborative work with preservation and restoration teams. This includes both analogue film contexts and hybrid analogue–digital workflows.

  • Handling and organisation of film elements in lab and archive environments.
  • Coordination between lab, archive and projection teams.
  • Participation in preservation-oriented projects that combine photochemical and digital steps.
